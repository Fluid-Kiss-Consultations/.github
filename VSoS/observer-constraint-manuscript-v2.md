## Abstract

Self-sealing reasoning structures in certain domains of theoretical physics are formally identical, not merely analogous, to documented cognitive mechanisms of delusional ideation — mechanisms characterized in clinical psychiatry and operating dimensionally across the population. I argue that the identity is not coincidence but a necessary consequence, given the architecture, of epistemic underdetermination operating on a causally bounded observer.

The argument proceeds across four layers. Contemporary neuroscience establishes that perception is constructive: the brain is a hierarchical prediction machine under a free-energy-minimization imperative (Clark 2013; Friston 2010). Clinical psychiatry has operationalized self-sealing belief under the Bias Against Disconfirmatory Evidence paradigm, with dimensional extension across nonclinical populations and integration of iterative and amortised inference (Woodward et al. 2006, 2007; Harding et al. 2024). Philosophy of science establishes that evidence underdetermines theory, so any theory can be maintained against disconfirmation by auxiliary adjustment (Duhem 1906/1954; Quine 1951; Stanford 2006). And within contemporary fundamental physics, the same structure is being formalized directly along four independent strands (Wolpert, Rovelli, and Scharnhorst 2025; Rovelli 2025; Wolfram 2023; Elshatlawy, Rickles, and Arsiwalla 2025).

No prior work bridges these literatures at this register. I argue the bridge is not merely available but required: the architecture that constructs reality and that theorizes about domains beyond direct causal mediation are the same architecture. When that architecture operates on domains where observation is possible only through translated causal residue, self-sealing reasoning is not a failure mode; it is the expected output. I illustrate by applying the framework to the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-quantum-mechanics debate (Hossenfelder and Palmer 2020; Adlam et al. 2024), showing it is diagnostic of the debate's structure without adjudicating its physics. Four reviewer objections — pathologizing, self-application, unfalsifiability, and paradigm-stability relabeling — are taken up in turn. The framework applies symmetrically across the landscape, including to this paper's author; recognizing the architecture does not free us from it, but naming it accurately may allow the community to make better-calibrated decisions about where progress is available and where it is not.

**Keywords:** observer-dependence; foundations of physics; epistemic underdetermination; predictive processing; Bias Against Disconfirmatory Evidence; Duhem-Quine thesis; superdeterminism

## §I. Introduction

Wolpert, Rovelli, and Scharnhorst (2025) recently argued that arguments in the Boltzmann-brain literature, together with the arguments standardly given for the Past Hypothesis and the second law of thermodynamics, contain circular reasoning that physics itself cannot resolve. Their central observation is sharp: the second law is used to establish that physical records are reliable, and the reliability of records is what we would need in order to establish the second law. They describe this as circular reasoning directly, and the Boltzmann-brain literature itself remains contested in the way their paper documents. The Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst result is one visible instance of a general structural pattern; the claim I defend names the pattern.

The claim is the following. Self-sealing reasoning structures in domains of theoretical physics where the underlying causal substrate is beyond the reach of direct causal mediation are formally identical to documented cognitive mechanisms of delusional ideation — mechanisms that have been empirically characterized in clinical psychiatry and shown to operate dimensionally across the population. By "formally identical" I mean isomorphic in the topology of how evidence routes through the system — a precise claim I defend in §VI. The identity is not a loose metaphor, and it is not a coincidence. It is a necessary consequence, given the architecture, of epistemic underdetermination operating on a causally bounded observer — an observer whose access to the world proceeds exclusively through causal mediation, whose cognitive architecture constructs rather than receives its model of that world, and whose protective machinery for maintaining belief under disconfirmation has been empirically characterized in clinical psychiatry.

The argument proceeds in four layers. Contemporary predictive-processing neuroscience shows that perception is generated rather than received, with sensory input functioning as error-correction against a top-down generative model (Clark 2013). Clinical psychiatry has operationalized a specific failure mode of that generative architecture — the Bias Against Disconfirmatory Evidence, or BADE — and has shown it to operate dimensionally across both clinical and nonclinical populations (Woodward et al. 2006, 2007). Philosophy of science establishes that evidence does not uniquely determine theory, so the protective machinery BADE describes is not merely a clinical pathology but a logically available option wherever theory choice is holistic (Duhem 1906/1954; Quine 1951). Fundamental physics, through multiple independent strands, has begun to formalize observer-dependent structure at its foundations, and the Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst result is one such strand. The paper then applies the framework to the live superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-QM debate as a worked case.

What follows is not a theory of physics. It is a diagnostic framework that clarifies the structure of a specific class of foundational debates and explains why those debates tend to stabilize into positions that resist updating from within themselves. The framework does not adjudicate whether superdeterminism, retrocausality, or standard quantum mechanics is correct. It does not refute any particular physical theory. It does not claim physicists are ill, and it does not exempt me from the architecture it describes. The cognitive architecture under analysis is the only one I have, and the framework applies to this paper as cleanly as it applies to any other. That consequence is not an embarrassment for the argument. It is the argument.

## §II. Layer 1 — The Observer as Constructor (Neuroscience)

The naive model of perception treats the brain as a passive receiver that transduces sensory input into representations of the external world. The model is old, intuitive, and wrong — or at least wrong enough that no serious account of perceptual neuroscience in the last two decades has defended it at its strong reading. What has replaced it is a family of models in which perception is generated rather than received: the brain constructs its best current hypothesis about the state of the world from the top down, compares that hypothesis to incoming sensory signal, and updates either the hypothesis or the weight assigned to the signal depending on which it estimates more reliable.

The clearest synthesis of this predictive-processing framework is Clark (2013). On this account, the brain is a hierarchical prediction machine: at each cortical level, top-down probabilistic models attempt to generate the sensory activity observed at the level below, and what propagates upward is not raw sensory data but prediction error — the residual signal where the generative model failed to match input. Perception is not the endpoint of a bottom-up inferential chain but the state of the generative model once the bidirectional cascade has settled into a minimum-prediction-error configuration. High-level regularities constrain intermediate ones, which constrain low-level ones; each level simultaneously predicts downward and sends error upward. Clark himself catalogues outstanding evidential, methodological, and conceptual challenges to the framework, and the argument here requires only its consensus-stable core: perception is constructed through a hierarchical predictive process, and prediction error is the signal that drives update.

Beneath this architectural description sits a more ambitious unifying claim. Friston (2010) argues that biological systems, including brains, operate by minimizing variational free energy — an information-theoretic quantity that provides an upper bound on the long-run surprisal of sensory samples. Perception, action, and learning fall under a single imperative: perception updates the model so predictions fit input; action changes input so it fits predictions; learning shifts the model's parameters so predictions track the world over time. The strongest readings of the free-energy principle are contested, with critics arguing they verge on the tautological, but the weaker reading — that the brain operates in large part through prediction-error minimization — is consensus-stable and sufficient here.

The predictive-processing picture might have remained a theoretical unification, open to the charge of being more philosophically congenial than empirically grounded. It did not. Ficco et al. (2021), aggregating Activation Likelihood Estimation data across seventy neuroimaging studies, identified the left anterior insula and the left inferior frontal gyrus as clusters appearing consistently in both prediction-encoding and prediction-violation conditions, with a broader predictive network extending through bilateral insula, frontal and temporal gyri, parietal lobules, and claustrum. The specific anatomy is not what matters for the argument. What matters is that the predictive-processing account is no longer merely a theoretical construct awaiting neural grounding; it is a framework with a localizable empirical signature that survives meta-analytic scrutiny.

This gives us the first of two premises the core claim requires: perception is constructed, not received. The second is more restrictive, and it is where the phrase "causally bounded observer" does its work. Every step in the construction process — from the transduction of physical stimuli into neural signal, through the propagation of that signal up the cortical hierarchy, through the downward flow of predictions and the updating of the generative model — is a causal step. There is no channel of perceptual or inferential access that bypasses this substrate; there is no modality of contact with the world that is not, at its lowest physical level, a causal contact. This condition is what I call causal mediation: the brain's access to the world proceeds exclusively through causal pathways, and whatever the brain ultimately represents is a reconstruction from the causal residue those pathways deposit. The thesis is physiological, not metaphysical. It makes no claim about the ultimate structure of reality. It claims only that reality, insofar as any observer has access to it, reaches that observer through causes — and that this fact sets the scope of what can and cannot be empirically adjudicated from within the observer's position.

Two consequences follow. First, any domain whose underlying substrate is not causally contactable in this sense is one the architecture can model only through translation, not through direct access. Second, when the architecture models such a domain, the same protective machinery that stabilizes perception against noise — the same precision-weighting that decides whether to update the model or discount the error — becomes available as a mechanism for stabilizing belief against disconfirmation. The next section takes up what happens when that machinery misfires, and why clinical psychiatry has been able to operationalize the misfiring as a measurable, dimensional cognitive phenomenon.

## §III. Layer 2 — When Construction Self-Seals (Psychiatry)

§II closed on two consequences of the predictive-processing architecture: that domains whose substrate is not causally contactable can be modeled only through translation, and that the precision-weighting machinery stabilizing perception against noise becomes available as machinery for stabilizing belief against disconfirmation. Where disconfirming evidence carries direct causal contact with the substrate being modeled, that second availability mostly stays dormant; disconfirming evidence arrives, is weighted as informative, propagates as prediction error, and the model updates. Where direct causal contact is absent, the architecture lacks that grounding pressure, and the second availability shifts from exception to default disposition. The question this section takes up is what the system does under those conditions — and why clinical psychiatry, studying the pattern in cases where direct causal contact *is* available, has characterized it in terms the framework needs.

The phenomenon has a name and a paradigm. Woodward et al. (2006) formalized what they called the Bias Against Disconfirmatory Evidence, or BADE, in a task where participants rate the plausibility of candidate interpretations of an ambiguous lead-in, receive additional information that either confirms or disconfirms their initial rating, and rate again. Participants with active delusions show systematically smaller adjustments after disconfirming information than control participants, while the corresponding effect on confirming information is substantially smaller. The disconfirming evidence is not missing, not misunderstood, and not denied in any behavioral sense. It is available to the participant, and their behavior relative to it does not update in the way a Bayesian-normative account predicts. The measurement is behavioral, the phenomenon is operationalized, and the pattern has been reproduced across samples and variants of the task. BADE is not a description of introspection; it is a coordinate on a scoring rubric.

Sterzer et al. (2018), representative of a broader family of predictive-processing accounts of delusion formation, integrate this finding into the architecture §II establishes. On their account, delusions arise when precision-weighting within the predictive-coding hierarchy becomes pathologically skewed — prior beliefs receive elevated precision, incoming prediction errors receive depressed precision, and the result is a generative model that resists update even when sensory or evidential signals would, under normative weighting, drive revision. The mechanism is not a novel failure mode grafted onto the normal architecture; it is the normal architecture with its weighting skewed past a threshold. BADE, on this reading, is not a distinct cognitive process but the predictive-processing system's evidence-integration machinery operating with a distribution of precision weights that is self-protective rather than self-correcting. The pathology is quantitative. Whatever produces a clinical case produces, at smaller magnitudes, the ordinary failure of an otherwise competent observer to update a model they have already settled on.

McLean et al. (2017) consolidate the empirical picture. Their meta-analysis of BADE and related evidence-integration impairments across studies in delusion-prone and psychotic populations finds a robust correlation: the magnitude of bias against disconfirmatory evidence tracks the severity of delusional ideation across samples, methodologies, and operational variants of the task. The effect is not an artifact of any one measurement instrument. It survives the kind of scrutiny that distinguishes signal from noise in clinical cognitive research. What Woodward et al. (2006) identified in a single paradigm, and what Sterzer et al. (2018) located within the predictive-processing architecture, shows up reliably wherever the measurement is attempted.

The move the framework needs is dimensional, not categorical. Woodward et al. (2007) tested BADE in nonclinical samples scoring high on delusion-proneness — individuals with no psychiatric diagnosis — and recovered the same pattern. The bias against disconfirmatory evidence did not appear only in clinically-ill populations with the trait maximally expressed. It appeared across the distribution, scaling with delusion-proneness as a continuous cognitive disposition. This is the finding that lets the framework apply without pathologizing. The mechanism is not a clinical curiosity present only in diagnosed individuals. It is a dimensional property of human cognitive architecture, elevated in clinical presentations but operative — at lower magnitudes, in narrower domains, under specific conditions — across the general population. Every cognitive system that runs the predictive-processing architecture §II describes has the precision-weighting mechanism available to misfire. Whether it misfires, and how strongly, and in what domain, is a separate question.

Harding et al. (2024) extend the account with a hybrid predictive coding model that distinguishes iterative from amortised inference. Iterative inference is the moment-to-moment error-driven updating §II described. Amortised inference is pattern-completion from a stored model — fast, feedforward, computationally cheap, and structurally resistant to local perturbation. The amortised component explains why entrenched beliefs are harder to dislodge than their evidential history alone predicts: they do not require iterative re-derivation each time they are drawn on, and disconfirming evidence must overcome not only current predictions but the cached commitments the amortised system supplies.

Structurally, BADE routes disconfirming evidence into explanatory auxiliaries — the evidence is reinterpreted within an expanded model, discounted against higher-precision priors, or localized to a source whose relevance can be contested — rather than permitting it to update the core. That routing pattern is not confined to clinical populations. It is cognitively available to any observer whose access to the target domain proceeds through causal mediation, whose construction process instantiates the predictive-processing architecture, and whose cognitive ecology affords competing explanatory resources. The cognitive availability is established here. Whether the same routing pattern is also logically permitted — independent of cognitive architecture, as a feature of evidence-theory relations themselves — is the subject §IV takes up.

## §IV. Layer 3 — Why It Is Always Formally Possible (Philosophy of Science)

§III closed with a specific partition: the evidence-routing pattern it described is cognitively available to any observer whose access to the target domain proceeds through causal mediation, whose construction process instantiates the predictive-processing architecture, and whose cognitive ecology affords competing explanatory resources. The question I take up here is whether the same routing pattern is also logically permitted — whether auxiliary-hypothesis deployment has structural cover independent of cognition, as a feature of the logic of confirmation itself, and not merely as a contingent feature of the architecture that happens to run on human brains. The answer philosophy of science gave well before cognitive science named the mechanism is yes. The holism of confirmation, in the form developed by Duhem (1906/1954) and generalized by Quine (1951), underwrites the routing as a property of evidence-theory relations. Not as permission-in-principle and failure-in-practice; as a logical structure that cannot be deleted without a corresponding revision of the account of how evidence confirms theory.

Duhem's observation about experimental testing in physics was that no hypothesis is tested alone. A scientist testing a claim against an experimental result tests it together with the assumed behavior of the instruments, the assumed absence of relevant interfering factors, the assumed correctness of the background theory that makes the measurement interpretable, and the assumed reliability of the specific auxiliary hypotheses linking the claim to an observable outcome. When the experiment disagrees with the prediction, logic alone does not tell the scientist which member of the tested bundle is at fault. The scientist may revise the core claim, or revise an auxiliary, or revise the background theory, or revise the calibration of the instruments, or revise some combination. Quine generalized the observation beyond experimental physics: any statement can be held true come what may, provided sufficient adjustments are made elsewhere in the web of belief. The holism is not license for irrational belief; it is a fact about the structure of confirmation. Evidence underconstrains the locus of revision because confirmation runs through networks, not through isolated statements.

The routing pattern §III described as cognitively available is therefore not an idiosyncratic feature of the predictive-processing architecture. It tracks a feature of how evidence bears on theory. An observer confronted with disconfirming information has, as a matter of logic, more than one place to put the revision; the holism of confirmation guarantees it. Which place the revision actually lands is a separate question, and depends on which auxiliaries the observer has cognitive access to, which revisions seem locally coherent with the rest of the observer's commitments, and which the observer's community will recognize as legitimate. The cognitive architecture selects from a structurally richer space than any single disconfirming result narrows. The Duhem-Quine move is not that disconfirming evidence never updates theory; it is that the update's location is not fixed by the evidence. This is the logical register of the routing pattern: what §III called cognitive availability has, sitting beneath it, a logical availability that the architecture does not manufacture but inherits.

Stanford (2006) gives the strongest single anchor for the section. Reviewing the history of theoretical science, Stanford documents a regularity: scientific communities confronted with a live explanatory question repeatedly fail to conceive viable theoretical alternatives that, from later vantage, turn out to be serious contenders for the truth. The failures are not distributed across eccentric fringe actors; they are systematic failures of the communities whose institutional role is precisely to conceive such alternatives. Stanford's new induction does not claim that theorizing is arbitrary, and it does not claim that any particular theory is under-supported against some specific rival. It claims something narrower and harder to dismiss: that the space of theoretical alternatives a community can generate is consistently smaller than the space of alternatives that turn out, historically, to be viable, and that there is no principled reason to think the present is an exception to the pattern. The claim reads back onto §§II-III. Stanford's argument is historical-epistemological; the framework reads the historical record through the cognitive architecture §II described. Theorizing is shaped by what the community's cognitive architecture can generate, and the architecture inherits the construction-and-precision-weighting features §II established. If the architecture is predictive, generative, and bounded, the space of theories it produces under any given epistemic condition is bounded by what its generative model can propose. Stanford's historical record is the empirical trace of that boundedness at the community level.

The framework does not require the strongest available form of the underdetermination thesis. The contemporary taxonomy distinguishes several — the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry (Stanford SEP), authored by P.K. Stanford, whose 2006 book anchors the previous paragraph, canonically maps the landscape — and Norton (2008) has argued persuasively that strong universal underdetermination, the claim that any theory is empirically equivalent to indefinitely many rivals, is harder to establish than often assumed; his arguments stand as the most rigorous contemporary skepticism about blanket underdetermination claims. Two responses are available, and I take the second. The first would be to engage Norton at the level of the universal claim and defend it; that is not this paper's argument. The second is to notice that the framework's argument does not need the universal claim. What it needs is a domain-conditioned version: that *when* a domain's substrate is inaccessible to direct causal contact — the translation-only-zone condition §III specified — the holism of confirmation is sufficient to establish that auxiliary-hypothesis routing is logically available. This is a weaker claim than Norton targets. Duhem-Quine holism, restricted to the translation-only zone and combined with the pattern documented in Stanford 2006 at the community level, is sufficient to underwrite the routing pattern §III describes. The framework concedes Norton's point and does not need what the point denies.

The cognitive and the logical converge. §II established the architecture that constructs rather than receives; §III showed that the architecture's precision-weighting machinery permits self-sealing as a cognitively available response under translation-only-zone conditions; §IV has shown that the same routing is logically permitted by the holism of confirmation, independent of the cognitive register, and that Stanford's new induction supplies the empirical record of the pattern at the community level. The two availabilities are not redundant. Cognitive availability without logical availability would imply that the routing survives only as a mistake the architecture makes despite the logic; logical availability without cognitive availability would imply that the routing is a possibility the architecture cannot actually execute. What the framework needs is both: a pattern the architecture can perform and a pattern the logic permits, such that the routing is neither a failure nor a curiosity but the structurally expected response to a specific epistemic condition. The question §V takes up is where, in contemporary physics, that condition obtains — and what the work looks like, at the foundations of the discipline, when it does.

## §V. Layer 4 — Observer-Dependence at the Foundations (Physics Case Study)

§IV closed on the question of where, in contemporary physics, the framework's condition obtains. The condition is specific: a zone in which the substrate being modeled is not available to direct causal contact for the observer, and in which the observer's inference about that substrate therefore proceeds through translation — through features the observer's architecture and community can construct rather than through the substrate itself. Contemporary fundamental physics contains at least three identifiable domains of this kind. The first is cosmological: the universe's initial macrostate, the Past Hypothesis, the thermodynamic arrow, and the Boltzmann-brain literature all concern conditions at which the observer cannot arrive by direct causal pathway, since the conditions are themselves constitutive of the pathways. The second is quantum-mechanical: the interpretational foundations of the theory — the measurement problem, the preferred-basis problem, the ontological status of the state vector — concern structure that, by construction of the formalism, is not available to empirical adjudication independent of theoretical commitment. The third is more general: proposals that derive physical law itself from the properties of observers rather than treating the observer as a peripheral feature of a pre-existing physical description. In each of these domains, the cognitive and logical apparatus §§II-IV developed is available; I take up here what the work looks like when it is.

Wolpert et al. (2025) provide the cleanest recent diagnosis in the first domain. Their paper argues that the standard arguments for the Past Hypothesis, for the reliability of physical records, and for the dismissal of Boltzmann-brain scenarios turn out to depend on one another in ways that physics-internal arguments have not disentangled. The reliability of records as evidence of the past requires a low-entropy initial condition; the low-entropy initial condition is inferred from records we take to be reliable; the dismissal of Boltzmann-brain scenarios requires a typicality measure that itself presupposes a privileged macrostate the Past Hypothesis is supposed to supply. Wolpert et al. characterize this as circular reasoning and are precise about which circularities chain through which arguments. What the framework adds is a structural reading. An observer whose access to the universe's initial macrostate is strictly indirect — i.e., through records and physical traces whose reliability is itself at stake — has no physics-internal resource to break the cycle; the ancillary commitments (the Past Hypothesis, typicality measures, the treatment of records as reliable evidence) are themselves the auxiliaries that the holism of confirmation makes available, and that a predictive-processing cognitive architecture operating in this domain will deploy. The circularity Wolpert et al. identify is, at the logical-structural level, the same routing pattern §III characterized at the cognitive-availability level and §IV underwrote as logically permitted. Cosmological reasoning about the thermodynamic arrow is a zone where the framework's conditions obtain, and the Wolpert et al. result is the shape the framework predicts.

Rovelli (2025) develops a converging thesis in a distinct key. His argument, framed as a return to Reichenbach, is that entropy and the thermodynamic arrow are not single quantities fixed by physics independently of any observer. They are quantities relative to a coarse-graining over degrees of freedom the observer does not and cannot distinguish, and that coarse-graining is a fact about the observer's physical situation, not about the universe in isolation. The arrow of time is observer-perspectival at the foundations; Reichenbach's branch-system account of time-orientation is underwritten by contemporary cosmology. Rovelli is a co-author on Wolpert et al. (2025), and the two works are related, but Rovelli 2025 pushes past the specific circularity result to the broader structural claim that thermodynamic description is irreducibly observer-indexed. On the framework's reading, Rovelli's observer-indexing is a physics-internal recognition, arrived at without any cognitive-science premise, that observer-dependence is structurally operative in the same zone §IV identified as translation-only. The physics literature is not merely compatible with the framework's condition; it independently formalizes the condition in its own register.

The third strand is more ambitious methodologically. Wolfram (2023), in a long-form essay that underwrites an ongoing observer-theory research program at the Wolfram Institute, argues that the specific form of the laws of physics we encounter — the dimensional structure of space and time, the statistical structure of quantum mechanics, the thermodynamic progression — are consequences of the observer being a particular kind of computational entity embedded in a much larger space of possible computational processes. Persistent, bounded, coherent-through-time computational observers of our kind reconstruct regularities of the form physical law takes; observers with different computational properties would reconstruct different regularities. The register is distinct from Wolpert et al. and from Rovelli: Wolfram is not formalizing a specific circularity or indexing a specific thermodynamic quantity, but proposing that physical law as the discipline writes it is, at the level at which it is written, already an observer-derived quantity. On the framework's reading, Wolfram's computational observer and the causally bounded observer of §II are instances of the same structural condition — observers whose access to a substrate proceeds only through construction, with the substrate accessible only through what the construction can represent. The argumentative register is different; the condition is the same.

Elshatlawy, Rickles, and Arsiwalla (2025) close the set. They construct a formal cybernetic theory of observers: a minimal observer is an agent with sensory channels into a world and actuator channels back out, described information-theoretically, modeled as a specific kind of coupled system within a physical or computational environment. The framework they build is explicitly physics-and-computational-science-side. It does not recruit predictive-processing or cognitive-science premises, and it does not claim that its formal observer models a human cognitive agent specifically; the apparatus extends across any physical system functioning as an observer in the sensor-actuator sense. Four strands, four methodologies. Wolpert et al. (2025) formalize a specific circularity in cosmological reasoning. Rovelli (2025) formalizes the observer-dependence of entropy. Wolfram (2023) proposes a laws-from-observer reconstruction of the specific form physical law takes. Elshatlawy et al. (2025) build a generalized formal theory of observers. None of these works develops the cross-disciplinary bridge §VI occupies. Each independently documents that observer-dependence is structurally operative at the foundations of physics, at precisely the zone the framework specifies.

What the four strands collectively establish is not that physics has arrived at the framework's thesis. It is that fundamental physics, independently of cognitive-science input and along four methodologically distinct routes, has converged on observer-dependence at the zone where direct causal contact with the substrate being modeled is unavailable. None of these works identifies the physics-side recognition with the cognitive architecture §II described, the psychiatric mechanism §III characterized, or the philosophy-of-science logical structure §IV developed. The cross-disciplinary bridge — the claim that what all of these literatures describe is structurally one phenomenon — is unoccupied in the literature. §VI takes up that bridge; it carries the argument the rest of the paper turns on.

## §VI. Synthesis

§V closed with the claim that the cross-disciplinary bridge — the claim that what §§II-V's literatures describe is structurally one phenomenon — is unoccupied in the literature. I take up that bridge here.

What §§II-V have each documented, within four methodologically distinct literatures, can be recast as instantiations of a single structural pattern. The recasting is the framework's move; the individual findings carry their own weight on their own terms. §II documents the predictive-processing cognitive architecture: an observer whose access to the world proceeds exclusively through causal mediation and whose model of the world is generated rather than received, with the same precision-weighting machinery that stabilizes perception against noise structurally available to stabilize belief against disconfirmation. §III documents that, where direct causal contact with the substrate is absent, this availability shifts from exception to cognitive default; clinical psychiatry has operationalized the underlying self-sealing mechanism as BADE and shown it dimensional across the population, studying the pattern in causally-contactable domains that the framework extends to the translation-only zone. §IV documents that the routing pattern §III describes as cognitively available is also logically permitted — the holism of confirmation (Duhem 1906/1954; Quine 1951) underwrites auxiliary-hypothesis deployment as a structural feature of evidence-theory relations, and Stanford 2006 supplies the community-level empirical record of the pattern. §V documents that fundamental physics, by four independent routes, has converged on observer-dependence at the zone where direct causal contact with the modeled substrate is unavailable — the circularity Wolpert et al. 2025 identify in cosmological reasoning, Rovelli 2025's observer-indexed thermodynamic arrow, Wolfram 2023's observer-theoretic reconstruction of physical law, and Elshatlawy et al. 2025's formal cybernetic observers.

The claim the framework makes is not that these findings are analogous. Analogies identify similarities and stop. The claim is that the four are instantiations of a single structural condition, and that the condition can be specified precisely. The condition is this: an observer whose access to a substrate proceeds exclusively through causal mediation, whose internal construction of that substrate takes the form of a generative predictive model, whose cognitive ecology supplies competing explanatory resources, and whose domain of inquiry does not admit direct causal contact with what the model refers to, inhabits an evidence-routing topology in which disconfirming evidence has structurally more than one locus of possible revision. The topology is a property of the access-plus-construction structure, not a property of any particular substrate or discipline. This is the condition the framework identifies, and §§II-V are what happens when it obtains.

§I previewed the claim that BADE-style self-sealing and theoretical-commitment entrenchment at the foundations of physics are formally identical, promising a precise defense in §VI. By formally identical I mean isomorphic in the topology of how evidence routes through the system, where "topology" names a specifiable structural object rather than a verbal characterization. The object is this: represent an observer's evidence-integration state as a directed graph in which nodes are propositional commitments (core theoretical claims, auxiliary hypotheses, background-theory presuppositions, instrumental-reliability assumptions, interpretive framing commitments) and edges are evidential-dependency relations — an edge from node A to node B indicates that A's evidential standing bears on B's, either by supplying support, by supplying disconfirmation, or by supplying a shared dependency through which evidential pressure propagates.

The core/auxiliary distinction is not primitive; it is a structural property of the graph. A node's centrality — its in-degree weighted by the aggregate evidential-standing of the nodes feeding it, with weight and standing mutually constituted in the holist picture §IV developed rather than fixed independently — locates it on a core-to-peripheral gradient: core commitments are those whose revision would propagate modification through the largest number of downstream dependencies, auxiliaries are those whose revision propagates locally. Disconfirming evidence enters the graph as a perturbation applied at a specific node (typically an observation-proximate auxiliary). Revision-locus selection is a graph operation: the observer identifies a node whose modification resolves the perturbation while preserving the maximum of prior aggregate coherence in the Duhem-Quine sense §IV installed — where revision's cost is the downstream modification it forces across the web of belief — and propagates the revision along the edges radiating from that node.

Two evidence-integration graphs are topologically identical in the sense §VI installs when there exists a bijective mapping between their nodes that preserves edge structure, preserves the centrality-gradient assignment of nodes to core-vs-auxiliary positions, and preserves the set of structurally available revision loci a given disconfirmation admits. The identity claim at §I ¶2 is, at this register, a claim that clinical-BADE evidence-integration and foundational-physics evidence-integration admit such a mapping. A patient with active delusions and a physicist defending a quantum-interpretation commitment, on this reading, are not merely instances of a general human fallibility they happen to share; their evidence-integration graphs admit the mapping, with the routing permitted logically by the holism of confirmation and executed cognitively by the predictive-processing architecture. What individuates them is not the graph structure but the substrate at which nodes are instantiated, the stakes attached to revision at each node, and the locus at which the revision comes to rest. The clinical case is individually diagnosable because psychiatric outcome measures make the locus visible; the physics case distributes the same graph structure across a community whose outcome measures are slower and more contested. The architecture is the same; the phenotype differs.

The framework specifies the object the identity claim is about; constructing the two graphs explicitly for clinical-BADE and for a selected foundational-physics debate, and verifying the bijective mapping, is work this paper does not perform. One further structural question the formal-verification program inherits is the scale-shift: the clinical graph is the evidence-integration state of an individual cognitive agent; the foundational-physics graph aggregates evidence-integration across a community of agents, each running an individual graph of their own. The framework's claim is that an isomorphism holds between the clinical graph and the community-aggregate graph at the abstraction level the specification installs, but the adequacy of the aggregation — which community-level structure counts as the node-and-edge reduction of the individual graphs, and under what conditions the aggregation preserves the isomorphism-relevant features — is exactly the kind of question formal verification would adjudicate. The specification names the object and the open questions; verification is a natural next step for the framework rather than a commitment of this section.

A reader encountering the claim will reasonably ask why, on this reading, theoretical commitments entrench as hard as they do. The logic of underdetermination, §IV makes clear, only *permits* the routing. The architecture of §II-§III makes the routing cognitively *available*. Neither alone explains the observed asymmetry in how difficult it is to dislodge an entrenched theoretical commitment compared to what the evidential history would predict. The missing component is in Harding et al. 2024. Their hybrid predictive-coding model distinguishes iterative inference — the moment-to-moment error-driven updating §II described — from amortised inference: pattern-completion drawn from a stored model, feedforward, computationally cheap, structurally resistant to local perturbation. Entrenched theoretical commitments, on the framework's reading, operate through the amortised component. A quantum interpretation is not re-derived each time it is deployed; it supplies the interpretive frame within which new data are already being read. Disconfirming evidence does not confront the commitment iteratively at first contact; it confronts the cached model that feeds forward before iterative processing begins. This is why the evidential history alone underdetermines the rate of entrenchment, and why §II-§IV are jointly necessary but §III's amortisation finding is the structural explanation of *how fast* the routing stabilizes.

The auxiliary-routing pattern has a specific signature in the quantum-interpretation literature, and the signature matches the topology §§II-IV specify. Disputes over the measurement problem, the preferred-basis problem, and the ontological status of the state vector are not adjudicated, in the main, by evidence that forces revision at a single locus. They are adjudicated by redistributing interpretive commitments across auxiliary structures — which entities count as ontologically primary, which measurement-outcome structure is fundamental, which invariance is preserved under which revision. The Everettian's response to Born-rule challenges, the pilot-wave theorist's response to Lorentz-invariance challenges, the objective-collapse theorist's response to the tail problem: each routes the pressure to a different auxiliary, preserves the central interpretive commitment, and does so coherently within the received holistic frame. This is not a diagnosis of quantum-foundations work as intellectually failing. It is a characterization of the structural mode in which the work, under translation-only-zone conditions, necessarily proceeds. §VII examines one contemporary case in detail: the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-QM debate, now formalized taxonomically (Adlam et al. 2024) and visible in Hossenfelder's own instrumentalist turn (Hossenfelder 2024).

A methodology note is due at this point of the argument. The framework treats each layer's empirically-anchored findings as instantiations of a common architectural pattern, and the identification of the four layers as instances of one structural condition extends beyond what any single cited source claims as a cross-disciplinary synthesis. The extension requires explicit acknowledgment. Across §§II-V I flagged eight synthetic moves — the translation-zone default-inversion generalization at §II para 1, the architectural generalization of Sterzer et al. 2018 at §III para 3, the auxiliary-routing characterization of BADE as Duhem-Quine-compatible across §III paras 6-7, the historical-record reading of Stanford 2006 through §II's predictive-processing architecture at §IV para 4, and four readings of physics-side results through the framework's condition at §V paras 2-5 (Wolpert et al., Rovelli, Wolfram, Elshatlawy et al.). Enumeration recites the ledger; aggregation by criterion is cleaner and no less honest. The criterion: each extension reads a layer's finding through the architectural apparatus the adjacent layer developed, and treats the finding as an instantiation of a structural condition that the cited source, on its own terms, does not claim as a cross-disciplinary pattern. The extensions are individually defensible on each layer's terms. The aggregate — the identification of the four layers as instantiations of one structural condition, with the topology of evidence-routing as the identifying invariant — is the framework's synthesis. The sources are not asked to carry that claim; they are asked only to carry what they individually assert. The graph-theoretic specification at ¶¶4-7 is the framework's specification of the invariant; it is not itself a synthetic move beyond the cited sources but a definitional sharpening of what the invariant is claimed to be.

What remains is the case. §VII takes up the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-QM debate as a worked application: a contemporary domain in which the translation-only-zone condition obtains, the generative-and-amortised cognitive architecture is operating at the community level, the holism of confirmation underwrites the auxiliary-routing the architecture executes, and the amortised-commitment machinery entrenches positions the evidence does not directly adjudicate. The framework does not adjudicate which of the interpretations is correct. It specifies what shape the debate necessarily takes, and reads Hossenfelder's instrumentalist turn as a recognizable move from within that shape rather than outside it.

## §VII. Application — Superdeterminism, Retrocausality, and the Standard-QM Debate

§VI closed by handing the application to §VII: a contemporary debate in which the translation-only-zone condition obtains, the generative-and-amortised cognitive architecture operates at the community level, the holism of confirmation underwrites auxiliary-routing, and the amortised-commitment machinery entrenches positions the evidence does not directly adjudicate. The case is the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-quantum-mechanics debate. Bell's theorem (Bell 1964) imposes a structural constraint on any local hidden-variable account of quantum correlations, and the constraint is not adjudicable by experimental violation alone: the inequality's derivation rests on a conjunction of assumptions — locality (or continuity of action), measurement statistics, and statistical independence — and an experimental violation of the inequality forces revision somewhere in the conjunction without specifying where. The SI arm of the conjunction carries its own foundational no-go result in Colbeck and Renner (2011): under the free-choice assumption, no extension of quantum theory can have improved predictive power. The theorem formalizes SI's load-bearing status at the foundational level — not as a derivational auxiliary of the Bell apparatus but as a structurally central commitment the Bell landscape relies on. The hidden-variable substrate, where one is posited, is by construction not available to direct causal contact: experiments yield correlation statistics consistent or inconsistent with the inequality, but the variables themselves are accessed only through the theoretical apparatus that posits them. The translation-only-zone condition obtains at the foundational level of the debate, not as a contingent feature of any single interpretive program. What the framework predicts under that condition is the pattern §§II-VI specify: structurally available revision loci, holistically permitted auxiliary deployment, amortised-commitment entrenchment of the central interpretive choice. What the contemporary debate exhibits is that pattern. The QM-interpretation case is not uniquely satisfying the condition at the physics-side foundations. §V documented four independent strands by which fundamental physics has converged on observer-dependence at the same translation-only zone — Wolpert, Rovelli, and Scharnhorst (2025) on the circularity underlying the Past Hypothesis and Boltzmann-brain reasoning at the cosmological register, Rovelli (2025) on the observer-indexed structure of entropy and the thermodynamic arrow, Wolfram (2023) on observer-theoretic reconstruction of the specific form physical law takes, and Elshatlawy, Rickles, and Arsiwalla (2025) on the formal cybernetic theory within which observers of the relevant kind can be characterized. Each strand occupies a different physics-side register; each independently identifies features of the condition the framework specifies. §VII takes up one case within that landscape; the framework's diagnostic reach — the translation-only-zone identification, the auxiliary-routing topology §§II-VI develop, and the amortised-commitment reading this section applies — extends, in principle, to each of the domains §V identifies, and what the rest of this section diagnoses for the QM-interpretation debate applies, with domain-specific translation, to the adjacent instances.

Each major camp in the debate preserves a different central commitment and routes the pressure of Bell's constraint to a different auxiliary. Standard quantum mechanics — across its interpretive variants — preserves statistical independence as inviolable and routes the revision into the interpretive layer: the measurement problem (collapse, decoherence, branching), the preferred-basis problem, the ontological status of the state vector, the tail problem in objective-collapse theories, the guidance equation and the Lorentz-invariance question in Bohmian mechanics. Each interpretation preserves its central commitment and absorbs Bell-derived pressure through structurally different auxiliaries. Superdeterminism (Hossenfelder and Palmer 2020) preserves locality and continuity of action and routes the revision to statistical independence itself: the joint distribution of hidden variables and measurement settings is held to be correlated in a structurally specifiable way, and the apparent acausality of Bell-type correlations dissolves into a feature of the joint distribution that no interaction needs to mediate at measurement time. Retrocausality, in the canonical reference Price (2012) supplies, preserves microscopic time-symmetry as the foundational commitment and routes the revision to the temporal direction of the causal arrow at the microphysical level, allowing future measurement settings to bear on present hidden-variable distributions without violating local dynamics. Each of these is a coherent response to Bell's constraint within the holistic frame; none is adjudicated against the others by the empirical record the constraint supplies. The topology of the debate is the topology §VI specified — same evidential pressure, multiple structurally available revision loci, the selection of locus underdetermined by the evidence the constraint provides.

The auxiliary the standard-quantum-mechanics camp preserves — statistical independence — is not, on the framework's reading, a peripheral or technical commitment. Statistical independence underwrites essentially all inferential practice in empirical science outside quantum foundations. Random sampling in clinical and survey research, treatment-arm assignment in randomized controlled trials, the assumption that experimental conditions and participant variables are uncorrelated unless specific mechanism is articulated, the operational design of essentially every controlled experiment in the natural and social sciences — all rest on statistical independence as a background commitment so deep it is rarely articulated, let alone defended, in the work that depends on it. Read through the §III-§VI architecture, statistical independence has the precise profile of an amortised inference at maximum precision-weighting: it is feedforward, computationally cheap, fed into downstream inference without iterative re-derivation, and structurally resistant to local perturbation because its revision would propagate into essentially every other inferential commitment the practitioner holds. Hossenfelder and Palmer (2020), and Hossenfelder in the public-argumentative register (Hossenfelder 2021), have identified this entrenchment pattern directly. Their diagnosis names the observable symptoms: systematic misattribution of SI-violation to loss of free choice of measurement settings, objections resting on classical-physics intuitions that do not transfer to the quantum case, and a community-wide pattern in which Bell's theorem is taught without the SI assumption being recognized as structurally separate from locality. The SI prior is defended, on Hossenfelder's reading, at a register the evidence does not reach. What the framework supplies, on this reading, is the cross-disciplinary architecture the diagnosis has been making without drawing on explicitly — the predictive-processing substrate §II establishes, the BADE mechanism §III operationalizes (dimensional across nonclinical populations per Woodward et al. 2007), and the amortised-inference specification Harding et al. (2024) supply — each of which is available to underwrite the rate-of-entrenchment claim the diagnosis rests on. The framework reads Hossenfelder's community-pathology diagnosis as correct; it extends the diagnosis symmetrically across the landscape the debate occupies and supplies empirical architecture, not independent observation. From there the framework predicts that the rate at which the community will revise such a prior, when it is challenged by structural argument from a domain (quantum foundations) the community treats as parochial, will be slower than the evidential history alone predicts; and predicts that the slowness will not register as such from within the architecture, because amortised commitments at this depth are not experienced as commitments but as the conditions under which inference happens at all. The framework's claim is not that any individual physicist is exhibiting the clinical-psychiatric mechanism §III operationalizes; the cognitive default in the translation-only zone is the framework's extension of §III's clinical finding, not the finding itself. The claim is that statistical independence functions in the contemporary community as a maximally entrenched amortised auxiliary, and that its persistence under structural challenge is the phenotype the framework's architecture predicts.

Two further data points concretize the picture. The first: a recurring dismissal of the superdeterminism position is that no concrete model exists, that the position is in-principle gesture rather than physics. Donadi and Hossenfelder (2022) construct a Lindblad-form toy model of local and deterministic wave-function collapse with explicit hidden-variable structure; the model reproduces Born's rule when averaged over the hidden-variable distribution and is structurally testable in low-noise small-system regimes. Whether the model is adequate as a theory of physics is not what the framework adjudicates. What the framework registers is that the empirical existence of the concrete model leaves the dismissal largely unchanged. The dismissal does not retreat to "the model is inadequate for reasons X, Y, Z"; it persists in the form "no serious model exists" or "the position is unscientific" alongside the model's continued availability. This is the entrenchment-rate phenomenology the amortised-commitment account predicts: the disconfirming evidence does not confront the prior iteratively at first contact but confronts the cached frame within which "serious model" is already adjudicated, and the cached frame survives the first-pass disconfirmation. The second: Adlam, Hance, Hossenfelder, and Palmer (2024) — the four-author Taxonomy paper that emerged from the 2022 Bonn workshop on physics beyond quantum mechanics — formalize the landscape of superdeterministic, retrocausal, future-input-dependent, and atemporal positions taxonomically, distinguishing model classes by structural features rather than treating the landscape as a monolithic departure from received foundations. The taxonomic move itself, on the framework's reading, is the pattern §VI characterized at community level: when auxiliary-routing reaches a density that resists evaluation as a single block, the discipline produces taxonomy as the structural acknowledgment that the routing is happening. Adlam et al. state explicitly that analyzing the origin of the unease their taxonomy surveys is not within their paper's scope; the framework this paper offers names that origin. Adlam et al. do not, in their paper, adopt the framework's diagnostic register, but they perform the move the register predicts.

§VI ¶9 anticipated the most provocative move §VII carries: Hossenfelder's instrumentalist turn (Hossenfelder 2024) read as recognizable-from-within the shape §VI specifies rather than as a position outside it. The reading requires care. Hossenfelder's recent position separates the question of what quantum mechanics describes (the interpretational layer the framework reads as auxiliary-routed) from the question of which empirical predictions the formalism delivers (the layer at which agreement is community-wide), and recommends, as a methodological discipline, declining to treat the interpretational dispute as a question physics is currently equipped to settle. The position is not a withdrawal from the superdeterminism advocacy; it is an explicit clarification of what the advocacy claims and what it does not. On the framework's reading, the instrumentalist turn is the structurally expected redistribution of weight produced at the translation-only zone when an observer — whether or not via the framework's diagnostic register — arrives at auxiliary-routing awareness: rather than commit further weight to a single interpretive frame whose adjudication the evidence does not supply, the observer narrows the load-bearing claims to what the empirical apparatus directly underwrites. This is recognizable from within the topology §VI specified — not a step out of it, because no such step is structurally available, but a redistribution of weight within the topology toward loci where the evidence does adjudicate. The framework reads this as a coherent move, not as a retreat. (Hossenfelder 2021 supplies the related clarification at the level of nomenclature: that statistical-independence violation does not entail loss of free choice of measurement settings — itself a translation-only-zone correction in which the load-bearing inferential commitment is being relocated from a misidentified auxiliary to its actual location.) The framework does not endorse Hossenfelder's interpretive program. It reads her methodological turn as a recognizable-from-within response to the topology she has been arguing within.

I close §VII with two clarifications. The first: the framework does not adjudicate which of standard quantum mechanics, superdeterminism, or retrocausality is correct. It does not supply evidential support for any of them, and it does not refute any of them. What it specifies is the topology of the debate — the structural sense in which each camp's response is coherent within the holistic frame, which auxiliary each preserves and which each routes pressure to, and why the community's rate of revision under structural challenge is what the amortised-commitment architecture predicts rather than what the evidential history alone would predict. §I's scope claim binds: this is a diagnostic framework, not a theory of physics. The reader most likely to mistake the framework for the latter is the reader of §VII, and the disclaimer is therefore most needed here. The framework also applies symmetrically across the landscape surveyed, including to this paper's author — per §I's scope clause, §VII's diagnostic reading applies to the present paper as cleanly as to any camp §VII diagnoses, since the cognitive architecture under analysis is the only one on offer; §VIII para 3 takes up the self-application question in the register it requires. The second: extending the framework to a contemporary live debate raises the obvious objections — that the diagnostic register pathologizes physicists, that the framework cannot itself be exempt from the architecture it describes, that the framework risks unfalsifiability, that what is being offered is a relabeling of paradigm-stability claims already present in the post-Kuhnian literature. §VIII takes these up.

## §VIII. Objections and Responses

§VII para 6 flagged the four objections extending the framework to a contemporary live debate naturally provokes: that the diagnostic register pathologizes physicists, that the framework cannot itself be exempt from the architecture it describes, that the framework risks unfalsifiability, and that what is offered is a relabeling of paradigm-stability claims already available in the post-Kuhnian literature. I take them in sequence.

The first objection, at its strong form, is not that the paper calls physicists ill — §I para 4 and §III para 5 foreclose that reading — but that the structural-isomorphism claim itself puts foundational-physics theoretical entrenchment in a category with delusional ideation, and no disclaimer at the individual-diagnosis level undoes the categorical placement. The framework concedes the isomorphism claim and specifies what it entails: shared topology of evidence-routing — core commitments standing in the same structural relation to auxiliary commitments, disconfirming evidence facing the same set of revision loci, locus-selection underdetermined by the evidence at the same mechanism. This topology is defined graph-theoretically in §VI ¶¶4-7, where nodes are propositional commitments, edges are evidential-dependency relations, and isomorphism is bijective node-mapping preserving edge structure and revision-loci set. It does not entail shared clinical status, diagnostic descriptor, or treatment relevance. §III para 3 establishes the foothold: the pathology is quantitative, not categorical — whatever produces a clinical case produces, at smaller magnitudes, the ordinary failure of an otherwise competent observer to update a model already settled on. Woodward et al. 2007 cash this empirically at the nonclinical register — the bias against disconfirmatory evidence is measurable across delusion-proneness as a continuous cognitive disposition, without any individual in the sample being clinically diagnosable. §VI ¶6 completes the move — the architecture is shared; what individuates the clinical from the community case is substrate, stakes, and timescale. §VII para 3 restates for the application: the community-level cognitive default is the framework's extension of §III's finding, not the finding itself. The topology-shared / substrate-different distinction is not rhetorical softening. It refuses two collapses the objection in either form would force: elision of the seriousness of clinical illness by collapsing the physicist's case into it, and elision of the framework's descriptive reach by shielding the physicist's case from it. The framework extends the architectural machinery, not diagnostic status.

The second is that the framework, applied to itself, refutes itself: if cognition in the translation-only zone exhibits the topology §VI specifies, this paper exhibits it too, and the argument collapses. §I para 4 closed with the response the framework holds to — the cognitive architecture under analysis is the only one available, the framework applies to this paper as cleanly as to any other, and the consequence is not an embarrassment for the argument but the argument itself. Self-application is not self-refutation. The framework makes a descriptive claim about the architecture and extends that claim to its own production rather than claiming an exemption it then forfeits. §I para 2's formulation makes the position explicit: the identity the paper defends is a necessary consequence, given the architecture, of epistemic underdetermination operating on a causally bounded observer. The framework describes the architecture operating under specified conditions, not a privileged observer-position from which the architecture is observed. Self-application is accordingly a prediction the paper makes about itself — the paper's own claims should exhibit the topology §VI specifies, and the question is whether the apparatus by which the paper's claims are made accessible to external scrutiny is equal to that prediction. What the extension requires is auditability at the layer-specific empirical register of §§II-V rather than the framework's unified register, since any attempt by the framework to self-audit from within its own unified register would be the move the framework diagnoses. The apparatus is visible in the published paper: §§II-V ground their claims in externally-verified empirical literatures (predictive-processing meta-analysis, BADE dimensional extension, Duhem-Quine holism and Stanford's historical record, four-strand physics-side observer-dependence convergence); §V para 5 names the cross-disciplinary bridge as the framework's specific contribution rather than any cited source's claim; §VI ¶10 explicitly acknowledges the synthetic moves. These are the published-paper operational substitutes for the self-exemption the framework declines.

The third is that the framework is unfalsifiable: any disciplinary pattern can be read as auxiliary-routing, any community resistance as amortised-commitment entrenchment. The framework's claims are physiological at source, not metaphysical — §II para 5 grounds the causal-mediation condition at the neuroscience register, not as a stipulation the framework imposes — and it is not an empirical theory of physics, so it does not carry falsifiability obligations in the register that objection typically invokes. It is scope-bounded by §I, §III para 1, §VII para 6, and §IX to the translation-only-zone condition, and within that scope it makes three structural predictions carrying specified negations. First: rate of theoretical-commitment entrenchment exceeds what a Bayesian-update counterfactual over the same evidential record, absent amortised-commitment entrenchment, would predict — the rate at which iterative inference alone, without the feedforward cached-model contribution Harding et al. 2024 document, would update. Candidate operational proxies — citation-pattern shift over specified time windows, review-paper synthesis-move density, mainstream-acceptance latency of peer-produced alternative models — situate the rate-claim for empirical follow-up; the claim itself is structurally specified, and observed rates at or below baseline would undermine it. Second: communities facing auxiliary-routing density beyond a threshold produce taxonomic formalization of the landscape rather than single-locus adjudication; Adlam et al. 2024 is an instance, and absence of the taxonomic move in such domains would undermine the claim. Third: redistribution of theoretical weight recognizable-from-within the topology is the structurally expected response at the translation-only-zone boundary, with §VII para 5's reading of Hossenfelder's instrumentalist turn as a concrete instance; its negation is a redistribution that exits the topology by appeal to direct-causal-contact evidence adjudicating at a single locus, and the framework predicts no such redistribution occurs within the scope it specifies. Stanford 2006's historical record — the systematic failure of scientific communities to conceive viable theoretical alternatives that later prove viable — supplies the community-level empirical trace the framework predicts the translation-only-zone condition generates, an independent observational anchor for the rate-of-entrenchment claim beyond the Harding et al. 2024 mechanism specification. The contrapositive anchored at §III para 1's polarity — direct-causal-contact domains exhibit rapid convergence of the kind the framework does not predict for translation-only zones — is observationally supported by ordinary experimental practice in synthetic chemistry, controlled pharmacology, and structural engineering. The four-strand independent physics-side convergence §V documents supplies a further empirical check, and the check is non-trivial: §V para 3 notes that the physics literature is not merely compatible with the framework's condition but independently formalizes it. Had fundamental physics, along four methodologically distinct routes, not converged on observer-dependence at the zone §IV identifies, the framework's claim that this is the structurally expected response would be undermined. The framework carries assessable predictions, and its scope-claim is testable against the contrapositive and against the physics-side literature independently.

The fourth is that what the framework offers is a relabeling of paradigm-stability claims already available in the post-Kuhnian literature. Kuhn (1962) characterizes community-level paradigm dynamics at a historical-sociological register; subsequent cognitive-science-of-science work (e.g., Nersessian 2008) has characterized paradigm stability at intermediate cognitive-psychological levels. The framework positions within that lineage by supplying the specific cognitive-architectural substrate those characterizations operate over — the predictive-processing generative model of §II, the self-sealing BADE mechanism of §III with its dimensional empirical foothold in Woodward et al. 2007, and the amortised-inference specification Harding et al. 2024 provides for rate of entrenchment. Kuhn's account gestures at cognitive-structural features — gestalt switches, paradigm-indexed training — that make paradigms sticky, but it does not specify the architectural substrate (predictive processing + amortised inference) that fixes the rate at which they stick, and it does not distinguish domains where the mechanism bites sharply (translation-only zones) from domains where it does not (direct-causal-contact practice). The translation-only-zone bounding §III para 1 installs is not available in Kuhn. The mechanism-specification §III para 3 supplies — Sterzer et al. 2018's architectural account of BADE as a distribution of precision weights, rather than a novel failure mode grafted on — distinguishes the framework's register from intentional-level accounts at a level Kuhn and the post-Kuhnian cognitive-science-of-science lineage have engaged obliquely. Intentional-level accounts (motivated reasoning, professional incentive, institutional pressure) generate rate predictions of their own via mechanisms distinct from the amortised-commitment architecture; the amortised-rate prediction the framework generates, by contrast, is not derivable from those supplementations of Kuhn's account, which would still need the architectural substrate the framework supplies to generate the rate-claim Harding et al. 2024 specifically underwrites. The cross-disciplinary bridge §§II-V integrate — predictive-processing neuroscience, clinical psychiatry's dimensional findings, philosophy of science's holism-plus-historical-record, and fundamental physics' four independent routes to observer-dependence — is the framework's specific contribution and is not available in Kuhn or in the post-Kuhnian cognitive-science-of-science literature individually. The framework is a subspecies-specification within a broader entrenchment phenomenon, mechanism-grounded at a level those literatures have not reached.

These four objections do not exhaust the pushback a contemporary reader may bring. They are the structurally load-bearing ones — the objections that, if not answered, defeat the extension §VII makes. Metaphor-not-formal, just-underdetermination, and realism-vs-antirealism objections are addressed implicitly by §VI ¶¶4-7, §IV paras 5-6 plus §VI ¶8, and scope-bounding at §I / §VII para 6 / §IX respectively. §IX crystallizes the framework's positive claim — what it is — and the negative-claim cluster §VIII paras 2-3 have pre-staged — what it is not — into closing form.

## §IX. What the Framework Is and Is Not

The framework is three things. It is, first, a diagnostic lens for recognizing a specific structural cognitive pattern in fundamental-physics theorizing: where direct causal mediation between the theorist and the target substrate is unavailable, the predictive-processing architecture that §II establishes makes available the auxiliary-routing and amortised-commitment entrenchment §III documents (per Harding et al. 2024's integration of iterative and amortised inference), the holism of confirmation §IV installs underwrites the structural permission for that routing, and the pattern is observable both in the contemporary foundations-of-physics landscape §V surveys along its four independent strands and in the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-quantum-mechanics debate §VII diagnoses. It is, second, a cross-disciplinary bridge between predictive-processing neuroscience, clinical psychiatry, philosophy of science, and foundations of physics — four literatures that do not currently cite each other at the register §§II-V integrate them, and whose integration is the framework's specific contribution per §V para 5 and §VI ¶10. It is, third, a reframing device: certain foundational debates are, on this reading, not soluble — from within the current architecture — technical disputes awaiting the right experiment or the right derivation but expressions of a structural epistemic condition — the translation-only-zone condition §III para 1 installs — within which the evidential record cannot adjudicate between the revision loci that the holism of confirmation makes structurally available.

The framework is correspondingly not several things. It is not a refutation of any particular physics theory; §VII para 6's first clarification installs the non-adjudication scope-binding, and §VIII para 4 specifies the structural predictions the framework does carry. It is not a claim that physicists are clinically ill or cognitively impaired; §III para 3's quantitative-not-categorical foothold, Woodward et al. 2007's dimensional empirical finding across nonclinical populations, and §VIII para 2's topology-shared / substrate-different distinction preclude the reading. It is not a claim that the paper's author escapes the architecture it describes; §I para 4 stipulates the symmetry, and §VIII para 3 develops the self-application-as-prediction-about-the-paper register the symmetry requires. It is not an endorsement of any position in the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-quantum-mechanics landscape; the framework survives any camp being right as cleanly as it survives any camp being wrong.

Recognizing the architecture does not free us from it. Naming it accurately may allow the community to make better-calibrated decisions about where progress is available and where it is not.
