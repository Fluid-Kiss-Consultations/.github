The framework is three things. It is, first, a diagnostic lens for recognizing a specific structural cognitive pattern in fundamental-physics theorizing: where direct causal mediation between the theorist and the target substrate is unavailable, the predictive-processing architecture §II establishes generates the auxiliary-routing and amortised-commitment entrenchment §III documents (per Harding et al. 2024's integration of iterative and amortised inference), the holism of confirmation §IV installs underwrites the structural permission for that routing, and the pattern is observable both in the contemporary foundations-of-physics landscape §V surveys along its four independent strands and in the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-quantum-mechanics debate §VII diagnoses. It is, second, a cross-disciplinary bridge between predictive-processing neuroscience, clinical psychiatry, philosophy of science, and foundations of physics — four literatures that do not currently cite each other at the register §§II-V integrate them, and whose integration is the framework's specific contribution per §V para 5 and §VI para 5. It is, third, a reframing device: certain foundational debates are, on this reading, not soluble technical disputes awaiting the right experiment or the right derivation but expressions of a structural epistemic condition — the translation-only-zone condition §III para 1 installs — within which the evidential record cannot adjudicate between the revision loci the holism of confirmation makes structurally available.

The framework is correspondingly not several things. It is not a refutation of any particular physics theory; §VII para 6's first clarification installs the non-adjudication scope-binding, and §VIII para 4 specifies the structural predictions the framework does carry. It is not a claim that physicists are clinically ill or cognitively impaired; §III para 3's quantitative-not-categorical foothold, Woodward et al. 2007's dimensional empirical finding across nonclinical populations, and §VIII para 2's topology-shared / substrate-different distinction preclude the reading. It is not a claim that the paper's author escapes the architecture it describes; §I para 4 stipulates the symmetry, and §VIII para 3 develops the self-application-as-prediction-about-the-paper register the symmetry requires. It is not an endorsement of any position in the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-quantum-mechanics landscape; the framework survives any camp being right as cleanly as it survives any camp being wrong.

Recognizing the architecture does not free us from it. Naming it accurately may allow the community to make better-calibrated decisions about where progress is available and where it is not.
