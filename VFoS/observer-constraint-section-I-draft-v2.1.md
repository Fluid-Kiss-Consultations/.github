---

Wolpert, Rovelli, and Scharnhorst (2025) recently argued that arguments in the Boltzmann-brain literature, together with the arguments standardly given for the Past Hypothesis and the second law of thermodynamics, contain circular reasoning that physics itself cannot resolve. Their central observation is sharp: the second law is used to establish that physical records are reliable, and the reliability of records is what we would need in order to establish the second law. They describe this as circular reasoning directly, and the Boltzmann-brain literature itself remains contested in the way their paper documents. The Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst result is one visible instance of a general structural pattern; the claim I defend names the pattern.

The claim is the following. Self-sealing reasoning structures in domains of theoretical physics where the underlying causal substrate is beyond the reach of direct causal mediation are formally identical to documented cognitive mechanisms of delusional ideation — mechanisms that have been empirically characterized in clinical psychiatry and shown to operate dimensionally across the population. By "formally identical" I mean isomorphic in the topology of how evidence routes through the system — a precise claim I defend in §VI. The identity is not a loose metaphor, and it is not a coincidence. It is a necessary consequence, given the architecture, of epistemic underdetermination operating on a causally bounded observer — an observer whose access to the world proceeds exclusively through causal mediation, whose cognitive architecture constructs rather than receives its model of that world, and whose protective machinery for maintaining belief under disconfirmation has been empirically characterized in clinical psychiatry.

The argument proceeds in four layers. Contemporary predictive-processing neuroscience shows that perception is generated rather than received, with sensory input functioning as error-correction against a top-down generative model (Clark 2013). Clinical psychiatry has operationalized a specific failure mode of that generative architecture — the Bias Against Disconfirmatory Evidence, or BADE — and has shown it to operate dimensionally across both clinical and nonclinical populations (Woodward et al. 2006, 2007). Philosophy of science establishes that evidence does not uniquely determine theory, so the protective machinery BADE describes is not merely a clinical pathology but a logically available option wherever theory choice is holistic (Duhem 1906; Quine 1951). Fundamental physics, through multiple independent strands, has begun to formalize observer-dependent structure at its foundations, and the Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst result is one such strand. The paper then applies the framework to the live superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-QM debate as a worked case.

What follows is not a theory of physics. It is a diagnostic framework that clarifies the structure of a specific class of foundational debates and explains why those debates tend to stabilize into positions that resist updating from within themselves. The framework does not adjudicate whether superdeterminism, retrocausality, or standard quantum mechanics is correct. It does not refute any particular physical theory. It does not claim physicists are ill, and it does not exempt me from the architecture it describes. The cognitive architecture under analysis is the only one I have, and the framework applies to this paper as cleanly as it applies to any other. That consequence is not an embarrassment for the argument. It is the argument.
