## I. Introduction (~560 words)

**Hook:** Open with Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst (2025). Contemporary physicists are now formally demonstrating that their own reasoning about the Second Law, the Past Hypothesis, and Boltzmann-brain arguments rests on circular choices that physics itself cannot supply. The authors name the circularity openly. The community has not converged on what to do with this observation. This is the live wall the paper addresses.

**Why this hook:** Physics-first framing disarms readers who would resist a psychiatric-first framing. The paper's thesis becomes more palatable once the reader has already accepted that physics is the thing that needs diagnosing. Psychiatric parallel enters as explanatory machinery, not as accusation.

**Claim statement:** Self-sealing reasoning structures in domains of theoretical physics where the underlying causal substrate is detached from direct observation are formally identical to documented clinical mechanisms of delusional ideation. The identity is not coincidence but a necessary consequence of epistemic underdetermination operating on a causally bounded observer.

**Preview of four layers + application** (one sentence each).

**Scope bounding:**
- Diagnostic framework, not theory of physics.
- Does not adjudicate interpretations of quantum mechanics.
- Applies symmetrically across the debate landscape, including to the present author.

**Citations in this section:** Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst 2025 (hook); skeleton references to Clark 2013, Moritz & Woodward 2006, Stanford 2006 (signposting).

## II. Layer 1 — The Observer as Constructor (Neuroscience) (~960 words)

**Purpose:** Establish that perception is generated, not received. This is the physiological grounding for the rest of the argument.

**Structure:**
1. The naive "perception as reception" model and its empirical failure.
2. Predictive processing (Clark 2013): hierarchical prediction machines; perception as prediction-error minimization in a bidirectional cascade.
3. The free-energy principle (Friston 2010): the mathematical substrate beneath predictive processing; minimizing variational free energy as a unifying imperative for perception, action, and learning.
4. Empirical meta-analytic support (Ficco et al. 2021): the predictive network as a defensible empirical finding, not just a theoretical posit.
5. The access modality: perception proceeds through causation. Neurons fire in causal chains; stimulus transduction is causal; every step from world to model is a causal step. We do not have a non-causal access modality. This is the bounding mechanism the paper's key phrase names.

**Load-bearing takeaway:** The brain does not receive reality. It constructs a model of reality, continuously tested against incoming causal residue. What we perceive is the model, not the substrate. The access modality is exclusively causal. This is a physiological fact, not a philosophical stance.

**Citations:** Clark 2013; Friston 2010; Ficco et al. 2021; Hohwy 2013 if useful as secondary.

## III. Layer 2 — When Construction Self-Seals (Psychiatry) (~960 words)

**Purpose:** Show that the construction process has a specific, empirically documented failure mode — the Bias Against Disconfirmatory Evidence (BADE) — with formal operationalization and dimensional extension across the population.

**Structure:**
1. Most of the time, the construction process works. The generative model updates on disconfirming evidence, predictions improve, perception tracks the world adequately.
2. Sometimes the updating does not happen. Clinical research has operationalized this failure mode: Moritz & Woodward (2006) — the BADE paradigm and its initial formalization.
3. Sterzer et al. (2018): predictive-processing integration of delusional ideation. Delusions as aberrant predictive processing — specifically, a breakdown in the weighting of prediction errors that normally drives model update.
4. Meta-analytic confirmation: McLean et al. (2017) — evidence integration impairment robustly associated with delusions across studies.
5. The crucial dimensional claim: Woodward et al. (2007). BADE operates across nonclinical populations scoring high on schizotypy. The mechanism is not confined to diagnosed psychosis; it is a dimensional cognitive phenomenon present across all brains.
6. Structural characterization of BADE: a generative model that routes disconfirming evidence into explanatory auxiliaries (the evidence is reinterpreted, discounted, or localized to an irrelevant source) rather than allowing it to update the core model.

**Load-bearing takeaway:** Self-sealing belief is not a metaphor. It is a specific, empirically documented, formally operationalized cognitive pattern, present dimensionally across the population, with known neural correlates in predictive-processing architecture. No member of the population needs to be clinically ill for the mechanism to operate.

**Citations:** Moritz & Woodward 2006; Woodward et al. 2007; Sterzer et al. 2018; McLean et al. 2017.

## IV. Layer 3 — Why It Is Always Formally Possible (Philosophy of Science) (~960 words)

**Purpose:** Establish the logical fact that underwrites the cognitive mechanism — evidence underdetermines theory, so any belief system can be maintained against disconfirmation via auxiliary adjustment.

**Structure:**
1. Duhem (1906) and Quine (1951): the holism of confirmation. Statements face experience as a corporate body. Any statement can be held true come what may by making sufficient adjustments elsewhere in the system.
2. Stanford (SEP, latest edition): the contemporary underdetermination landscape; authoritative contemporary survey.
3. Stanford (2006), *Exceeding Our Grasp*: the new induction. Scientific communities have repeatedly failed to conceive theoretical alternatives that later proved viable. Theorizing is shaped by the cognitive architecture's capacity to generate hypotheses.
4. Norton (2008) acknowledged: strong universal underdetermination is not required for the paper's argument. The Quine-Duhem holism plus Stanford's empirical-historical new induction suffice. Citing Norton here as the strongest modern critic against whom the weaker argument is resistant.
5. The bridge to Layers 1-2: underdetermination is not just a logical fact. It is the structural condition a causally bounded observer faces whenever their access to the target domain is mediated by translation. What distinguishes productive scientific reasoning from BADE-dominated reasoning is not a different logical structure. It is a community-level disposition toward updating plus substrate-level access that permits updating to occur.

**Load-bearing takeaway:** Underdetermination is logically ineliminable. Any observer facing it — whether a clinical patient defending a delusion or a physicist defending an interpretation — has access to the same protective machinery: auxiliary adjustment. The machinery's availability does not distinguish science from delusion. What distinguishes them is whether the relevant auxiliaries can be cross-examined against independent access to the substrate. When independent access is not available, the two kinds of reasoning become formally identical.

**Citations:** Quine 1951; Duhem 1906; Stanford 2006; Stanford SEP; Norton 2008.

## V. Layer 4 — The Structure in Physics (Case Study) (~1,200 words)

**Purpose:** Demonstrate that contemporary fundamental physics is now openly encountering the structural wall. Establish the priority claim — that the cross-disciplinary bridge to Layers 1-2 is unoccupied.

**Structure:**
1. Wolpert, Rovelli, and Scharnhorst (2025): the foundational case. Formalization of the circularity in Second-Law / Past-Hypothesis / Boltzmann-brain reasoning. These arguments rely on arbitrary conditioning choices that physics itself cannot supply. The authors are explicit about the structure; they do not connect it to cognitive science.
2. Wolfram (2023) "Observer Theory": laws of physics as derivable from observer properties (computational boundedness, persistence-in-time assumption). Physics as observer-relative. Flagged caveat: long-form essay on personal site, not peer-reviewed; cite with the Wolfram Institute observer-theory research program as secondary anchor.
3. Arsiwalla, Elshatlawy, and Rickles (2025) "Towards a Generalized Theory of Observers": formal cybernetic / sensor-actuator framework; observers as the partitions that make physics tractable. Closest-in-theme work to this paper. Operates entirely within physics / computational / cybernetic terrain. Does not cross into predictive-processing neuroscience or psychiatric literature.
4. Rovelli (2024) "Back to Reichenbach": sustained position on observer-dependent entropy across Rovelli's work.
5. The priority claim. None of the Layer 4 physics works crosses into Layers 1-2. None of the Layer 1-2 cognitive-science works applies to fundamental physics theorizing. The cross-disciplinary bridge is unoccupied. Arsiwalla et al. is the closest in theme; this paper occupies the distinctive structural position of bridging the physics-side observer-theory program to the cognitive-architecture side that grounds it physiologically.

**Load-bearing takeaway:** Physics has walked up to the wall. Multiple authors working independently have formalized observer-dependent structure in fundamental physics, and one author team (Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst) has formalized the circularity directly. None of them has named what the wall is. The next section names it.

**Citations:** Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst 2025; Wolfram 2023; Arsiwalla et al. 2025; Rovelli 2024.

## VI. Synthesis — One Architecture, Four Views (~1,200 words)

**Purpose:** The argumentative core. Show that the four layers describe one phenomenon seen from four angles, and make the formal-identity claim deductively rather than suggestively.

**Structure:**
1. The central claim: the cognitive architecture doing perception (Layer 1), the one doing clinical reasoning (Layer 2), the one doing philosophy of science (Layer 3), and the one doing fundamental physics (Layer 4) are the same architecture.
2. Two features of that architecture: (a) construction, not reception; (b) operation under underdetermination.
3. When the architecture is confronted with a domain where the underlying substrate is detached from direct observation — non-causal, non-medium-scale, or accessible only through translation — it faces a structural condition. Any model reproducing the observable residue is formally equivalent to any other. Disconfirmation from within cannot proceed.
4. The architecture's response to this condition is not failure; it is rational. Maintain the current model. Deploy auxiliary hypotheses to absorb anomalies. Restrict claims to instrumental prediction. Route disconfirming queries out of the theory's scope.
5. **What formal identity means here** (expanded beat, funded by the +240 redistribution): The claim is not that BADE-maintained delusion and fundamental-physics theorizing *feel* similar or have similar surface patterns. The claim is that the topology of evidence-routing is the same. In BADE: disconfirming evidence is reinterpreted, discounted, or localized to an auxiliary source rather than updating the core model (Woodward et al. 2007; Sterzer et al. 2018). In QM interpretation: disconfirming evidence is reinterpreted (as measurement artifact), discounted (as decoherence noise), or localized to an auxiliary source (hidden variables, the environment, the observer's position in the ruliad) rather than updating the core interpretive commitment. The evidence-routing topology is structurally isomorphic. Layer 1 explains why the architecture does this (prediction-error minimization, free-energy reduction). Layer 3 explains why the architecture *can* do this and remain self-consistent (Quine-Duhem holism). Layer 4 shows the architecture in the act.
6. This response, in clinical populations, is called BADE. In physics, it is called normal theoretical work. The distinction is between whether the substrate permits updating (architecture updates, we call it science) or whether it does not (architecture protects, we call it delusion — unless the theorist has institutional standing, in which case we call it foundational work). The distinction is not about the architecture's operation; the architecture operates identically in both cases. The distinction is about the epistemic conditions the architecture is operating under.
7. The claim is formal identity, not metaphor. The shape of the reasoning is the same because the architecture generating it is the same, and the structural conditions producing self-sealing are the same (construction + underdetermination + no independent access for cross-examination). This is not a claim that can be argued away by finding cases where physics and clinical delusion differ — of course they differ in content, stakes, institutional context, and social recognition. The claim is that the shape of the reasoning under the structural condition of non-causal access is the same shape, and this is a consequence of shared substrate, not of any particular physicist's or any particular patient's character.
8. The instrumentation question: instruments designed to translate non-causal or indirect domains into causal readouts are designed, built, and interpreted by causally bounded minds. The output is a causal shadow of the target substrate. Whether the shadow faithfully represents the substrate is not a question the architecture can answer from within itself. The shadow is the data; the data are interpreted by the architecture that generated them. Instrumentation does not escape the cage; it translates the cage's limits into the only modality the cage has available.

**Load-bearing takeaway:** The framework is not claiming that physics is delusional. It is claiming that the cognitive substrate common to all human theorizing exhibits a specific, documented structural pattern when it operates on domains beyond direct causal access. Physics and clinical delusion are two deployments of the same substrate under different epistemic conditions. The structural similarity is not a coincidence; it is a consequence of shared architecture operating under a shared structural condition.

**Citations:** Cross-referencing Layers 1-4; Woodward et al. 2007 and Sterzer et al. 2018 named again in item 5 as evidence for the topological isomorphism. No new primary sources expected.

## VII. Application — The Superdeterminism Debate (~1,360 words)

**Purpose:** Demonstrate the framework's diagnostic utility on a concrete live debate. Bounded scope; not advocating any position.

**Structure:**
1. Framing. The contemporary quantum-foundations debate — standard QM, superdeterminism, retrocausality — is not a soluble technical dispute. It is the framework's prediction made visible. Each camp protects its prior (locality, time-symmetry, Statistical Independence) via auxiliary machinery that becomes unfalsifiable within its own framework.
2. Brief landscape survey:
   - Standard QM accepts nonlocality and denies the need for hidden variables (Bell's theorem as closure).
   - Superdeterminism relaxes Statistical Independence to preserve locality and determinism (Hossenfelder & Palmer 2020; Donadi & Hossenfelder 2022).
   - Retrocausality relaxes temporal asymmetry of causation to preserve locality (single-line seeded reference to Price 2012, TSAR argument).
3. Hossenfelder's community-pathology diagnosis. She is correct that SI-violation is systematically misattributed to loss of free will; correct that objections rely on classical-physics intuitions; correct that most physicists don't recognize Bell's theorem requires SI as a separate assumption from locality (Hossenfelder 2021 Backreaction blog; Hossenfelder 2024 PIRSA). The framework extends her diagnosis: the same structural mechanism she identifies in the community's reception of superdeterminism applies symmetrically across the entire landscape, including to her own program, and including to the present author.
4. Concrete illustration: Donadi-Hossenfelder 2022. A Lindblad-form master equation with future-input dependence that reproduces Born's rule when averaged over uniformly-distributed hidden variables. The model exhibits the three structural moves the framework identifies:
   - **Move 1 (assumption-protection):** "The assumptions of a theory are never explained within the theory itself." The question of how hidden variables correlate with future detector settings is routed out of the theory's scope rather than answered.
   - **Move 2 (meta-physical / instrumental split):** Fine-tuning objections are reclassified as meta-physical (optional) rather than instrumental (binding), neutralizing them without engaging their object-level content.
   - **Move 3 (testability-by-design):** The authors explicitly acknowledge the model is "strictly speaking, untestable" without additional theoretical machinery that does not yet exist.
   These moves are not errors or intellectual failings. They are exactly what the framework predicts any serious theorist defending a theory in this terrain to do. Donadi-Hossenfelder name the limitations openly, which is scholarly honesty of a high order.
5. The Taxonomy paper (Adlam, Hance, Hossenfelder, Palmer 2024). The formal peer-reviewed synthesis from the 2022 Bonn Workshop on Superdeterminism and Retrocausality. Classifies superdeterministic (strong/weak), retrocausal (with/without signalling, dynamical/non-dynamical), future-input-dependent, atemporal, and all-at-once models under a unified framework. Framework-relevant observation: the Taxonomy paper states explicitly that analyzing "the origin of this unease" is not its purpose. The framework this paper offers names that origin.
6. The instrumentalist move. Hossenfelder (2024) "Quantum Confusions, Cleared Up" opens by declaring a purely instrumentalist stance. The framework reads this as a rational and structurally expected response — an instrumentalist frame releases the theorist from realist commitments that cannot be adjudicated from within a causally bounded observer's position. It is not a weakness of her position; it is a structural feature of any position occupying this epistemic location.
7. What the framework does and does not do here. Diagnostic of the debate's structure, not adjudicating its physics. The framework is silent on whether superdeterminism, retrocausality, or standard QM is correct. It explains why the debate has the shape it has, why the shape persists, and why it may not be resolvable from within the current architecture. The framework survives any camp being right as cleanly as it survives any camp being wrong.

**Citations:** Hossenfelder & Palmer 2020; Donadi & Hossenfelder 2022; Adlam/Hance/Hossenfelder/Palmer 2024; Hossenfelder 2024 (Quantum Confusions); Hossenfelder 2021 (Backreaction); Hossenfelder 2024 (PIRSA); Price 2012 (single-line seeded).

## VIII. Objections and Responses (~640 words)

**Purpose:** Anticipate and address the most obvious reviewer pushback.

**Structure:** Five objections, each with a compact response.

1. **"The parallel is metaphorical, not formal."** Response: BADE is empirically operationalized with specific measurable cognitive markers — prediction-error signal processing, evidence integration impairment — with neural correlates and meta-analytic support. Underdetermination is formally characterized via Quine-Duhem holism. The isomorphism is between these two formal characterizations, not between handwaved concepts.

2. **"You're just saying underdetermination. That's old news."** Response: The novel claim is the cross-disciplinary bridge. Underdetermination establishes logical possibility; this framework establishes cognitive-architectural necessity. Philosophy of science says self-sealing reasoning is logically permitted; this framework says it is structurally expected, and explains what specific mechanism generates it, and documents the mechanism's empirical signatures.

3. **"Physicists are not clinical patients. The comparison is insulting."** Response: The framework does not claim physicists are ill. It claims the cognitive architecture all brains share exhibits, dimensionally (Woodward et al. 2007), the documented mechanism. Applied to domains where causal updating is not available, the mechanism produces self-sealing reasoning. No member of the community needs to be diagnosable for the claim to hold.

4. **"If your framework applies to the framework's author, doesn't it refute itself?"** Response: No. The framework does not claim to escape the architecture. It claims to describe it. The description may itself be self-sealing in the way the framework predicts — the framework acknowledges this openly. Its truth-value is not the question. Its diagnostic utility is the question, and that is assessed by whether the framework produces useful predictions about the debate's structure.

5. **"This is just scientific realism vs anti-realism, repackaged."** Response: The framework is agnostic on scientific realism. A realist can accept the framework by saying the architecture sometimes tracks truth and sometimes does not; an anti-realist can accept it by saying the architecture never tracks anything beyond instrumental utility. The framework does not arbitrate. It describes a structural pattern; what the pattern implies about ontology is a separate question.

**Citations:** Minimal new citations in this section; mostly cross-referencing.

## IX. What the Framework Is and Is Not (~160 words)

**Purpose:** Closure. Bounded claim-stating and honest humility.

**Structure:** Two short paragraphs.

1. What it is:
   - A diagnostic lens for recognizing a structural cognitive pattern in fundamental-physics theorizing.
   - A cross-disciplinary bridge between predictive-processing neuroscience, clinical psychiatry, philosophy of science, and foundations of physics.
   - A reframing device: certain foundational debates are not soluble technical disputes but expressions of a structural epistemic condition.

2. What it is not:
   - A refutation of any particular physics theory.
   - A claim that physicists are ill or cognitively impaired.
   - A claim that the present author escapes the architecture.
   - An endorsement of any position in the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-QM landscape.

3. Closing sentence: Recognizing the architecture does not free us from it. Naming it accurately may allow the community to make better-calibrated decisions about where progress is available and where it is not.
