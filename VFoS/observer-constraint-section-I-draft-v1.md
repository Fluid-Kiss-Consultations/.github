# §I. Introduction — Draft v1

**Paper:** "The Observer-Constraint Framework" (working title, keystone paper #5)
**Author:** mourne / John A. Welch
**Section target:** ~560 words (7% of ~8,000-word paper)
**This draft:** ~570 words
**Citations used:** Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst 2025; Clark 2013; Woodward et al. 2006; Stanford 2006

---

Wolpert, Rovelli, and Scharnhorst (2025) recently formalized a structural observation that the Second Law, the Past Hypothesis, and Boltzmann-brain arguments in contemporary physics rely on circular choices of conditioning events that physics itself cannot supply. Their paper names the circularity openly. It is a clean demonstration that at least one class of foundational physical reasoning — reasoning about the origin and direction of thermodynamic asymmetry — cannot be grounded from within physics. The community has not converged on what to do with this observation. This paper begins from the premise that the observation is correct, that its scope is wider than the specific cases Wolpert and colleagues treat, and that the community's difficulty in responding to it is itself diagnostic.

The claim I defend is the following. Self-sealing reasoning structures in domains of theoretical physics where the underlying causal substrate is detached from direct observation are formally identical to documented clinical mechanisms of delusional ideation. The identity is not a loose metaphor, and it is not a coincidence. It is a necessary consequence of epistemic underdetermination operating on a causally bounded observer — an observer whose access to the world proceeds exclusively through causal transduction, whose cognitive architecture constructs rather than receives its model of that world, and whose protective machinery for maintaining belief under disconfirmation has been empirically characterized in clinical psychiatry.

The argument proceeds in four layers. Contemporary predictive-processing neuroscience shows that perception is generated rather than received, with sensory input functioning as error-correction against a top-down generative model (Clark 2013). Clinical psychiatry has operationalized a specific failure mode of that generative architecture — the Bias Against Disconfirmatory Evidence, or BADE — as a dimensional cognitive phenomenon documented across both clinical and nonclinical populations (Woodward et al. 2006). Philosophy of science establishes that evidence formally underdetermines theory, so the protective machinery BADE describes is not merely a clinical pathology but a logically available option wherever theory choice is holistic (Stanford 2006). Fundamental physics, through multiple independent strands, has begun to formalize observer-dependent structure at the foundations of the discipline, and the Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst result is one instance. The paper then applies the framework to the live superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-QM debate as a worked case.

What follows is not a theory of physics. It is a diagnostic framework that clarifies the structure of a specific class of foundational debates and explains why those debates tend to stabilize into positions that resist updating from within themselves. The framework does not adjudicate whether superdeterminism, retrocausality, or standard quantum mechanics is correct. It does not refute any particular physical theory. It does not claim physicists are ill, and it does not exempt the present author from the architecture it describes. The cognitive architecture under analysis is the only one I have, and the framework applies to this paper as cleanly as it applies to any other. That consequence is not an embarrassment for the argument. It is the argument.

**Structural choices made:**

- **Four paragraphs** rather than more. The outline has four content buckets (hook, claim, preview, scope); each gets its own paragraph. Avoids the introduction-as-a-list-of-bullet-paragraphs anti-pattern some papers drift into.
- **First "I" appears in para 2** ("The claim I defend is the following"). This is where single-author voice establishes itself — at the moment of the load-bearing thesis statement. Subsequent "I"/"the present author" usage is restrained.
- **Hook paragraph's final sentence** ("the community's difficulty in responding to it is itself diagnostic") is the pivot from descriptive observation to diagnostic claim. It seeds §VI without naming it.
- **Para 2's triple structure** ("causal transduction / constructs rather than receives / empirically characterized in clinical psychiatry") is a compressed preview of Layers 1 and 2. Layer 3 appears in para 3 with Stanford; Layer 4 closes para 3 with Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst.
- **Scope-bounding paragraph's closing** ("That consequence is not an embarrassment for the argument. It is the argument.") serves the §IX voice work at the opening rather than reserving it entirely for the close. Two registers bookending the paper.

- "causally bounded observer" (canonical from abstract).
- "detached from direct observation" (as distinct from "beyond observation" — the paper's position is that indirect causal shadows are detachments, not absences).
- "formally identical" (not "structurally similar," not "parallel" — identity is the load-bearing claim).
- "That consequence is not an embarrassment for the argument. It is the argument." (the closer, doing §IX voice work.)
