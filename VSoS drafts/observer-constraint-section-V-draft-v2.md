§IV closed on the question of where, in contemporary physics, the framework's condition obtains. The condition is specific: a zone in which the substrate being modeled is not available to direct causal contact for the observer, and in which the observer's inference about that substrate therefore proceeds through translation — through features the observer's architecture and community can construct rather than through the substrate itself. Contemporary fundamental physics contains at least three identifiable domains of this kind. The first is cosmological: the universe's initial macrostate, the Past Hypothesis, the thermodynamic arrow, and the Boltzmann-brain literature all concern conditions at which the observer cannot arrive by direct causal pathway, since the conditions are themselves constitutive of the pathways. The second is quantum-mechanical: the interpretational foundations of the theory — the measurement problem, the preferred-basis problem, the ontological status of the state vector — concern structure that, by construction of the formalism, is not available to empirical adjudication independent of theoretical commitment. The third is more general: proposals that derive physical law itself from the properties of observers rather than treating the observer as a peripheral feature of a pre-existing physical description. In each of these domains, the cognitive and logical apparatus §§II-IV developed is available; I take up here what the work looks like when it is.

Wolpert et al. (2025) provide the cleanest recent diagnosis in the first domain. Their paper argues that the standard arguments for the Past Hypothesis, for the reliability of physical records, and for the dismissal of Boltzmann-brain scenarios turn out to depend on one another in ways that physics-internal arguments have not disentangled. The reliability of records as evidence of the past requires a low-entropy initial condition; the low-entropy initial condition is inferred from records we take to be reliable; the dismissal of Boltzmann-brain scenarios requires a typicality measure that itself presupposes a privileged macrostate the Past Hypothesis is supposed to supply. Wolpert et al. characterize this as circular reasoning and are precise about which circularities chain through which arguments. What the framework adds is a structural reading. An observer whose access to the universe's initial macrostate is strictly indirect — i.e., through records and physical traces whose reliability is itself at stake — has no physics-internal resource to break the cycle; the ancillary commitments (the Past Hypothesis, typicality measures, the treatment of records as reliable evidence) are themselves the auxiliaries that the holism of confirmation makes available, and that a predictive-processing cognitive architecture operating in this domain will deploy. The circularity Wolpert et al. identify is, at the logical-structural level, the same routing pattern §III characterized at the cognitive-availability level and §IV underwrote as logically permitted. Cosmological reasoning about the thermodynamic arrow is a zone where the framework's conditions obtain, and the Wolpert et al. result is the shape the framework predicts.

Rovelli (2025) develops a converging thesis in a distinct key. His argument, framed as a return to Reichenbach, is that entropy and the thermodynamic arrow are not single quantities fixed by physics independently of any observer. They are quantities relative to a coarse-graining over degrees of freedom the observer does not and cannot distinguish, and that coarse-graining is a fact about the observer's physical situation, not about the universe in isolation. The arrow of time is observer-perspectival at the foundations; Reichenbach's branch-systems move is recovered in contemporary formalism. Rovelli is a co-author on Wolpert et al. (2025), and the two works are related, but Rovelli 2025 pushes past the specific circularity result to the broader structural claim that thermodynamic description is irreducibly observer-indexed. On the framework's reading, Rovelli's observer-indexing is a physics-internal recognition, arrived at without any cognitive-science premise, that observer-dependence is structurally operative in the same zone §IV identified as translation-only. The physics literature is not merely compatible with the framework's condition; it independently formalizes the condition in its own register.

The third strand is more ambitious methodologically. Wolfram (2023), in a long-form essay that underwrites an ongoing observer-theory research program at the Wolfram Institute, argues that the specific form of the laws of physics we encounter — the dimensional structure of space and time, the statistical structure of quantum mechanics, the thermodynamic progression — are consequences of the observer being a particular kind of computational entity embedded in a much larger space of possible computational processes. Persistent, bounded, coherent-through-time computational observers of our kind reconstruct regularities of the form physical law takes; observers with different computational properties would reconstruct different regularities. The register is distinct from Wolpert et al. and from Rovelli: Wolfram is not formalizing a specific circularity or indexing a specific thermodynamic quantity, but proposing that physical law as the discipline writes it is, at the level at which it is written, already an observer-derived quantity. On the framework's reading, Wolfram's computational observer and the causally bounded observer of §II are instances of the same structural condition — observers whose access to a substrate proceeds only through construction, with the substrate accessible only through what the construction can represent. The argumentative register is different; the condition is the same.

Arsiwalla et al. (2025) close the set. They construct a formal cybernetic theory of observers: a minimal observer is an agent with sensory channels into a world and actuator channels back out, described information-theoretically, modeled as a specific kind of coupled system within a physical or computational environment. The framework they build is explicitly physics-and-computational-science-side. It does not recruit predictive-processing or cognitive-science premises, and it does not claim that its formal observer models a human cognitive agent specifically; the apparatus extends across any physical system functioning as an observer in the sensor-actuator sense. Four strands, four methodologies. Wolpert et al. (2025) formalize a specific circularity in cosmological reasoning. Rovelli (2025) formalizes the observer-dependence of entropy. Wolfram (2023) proposes a laws-from-observer reconstruction of the specific form physical law takes. Arsiwalla et al. (2025) build a generalized formal theory of observers. None of these works develops the cross-disciplinary bridge §VI occupies. Each independently documents that observer-dependence is structurally operative at the foundations of physics, at precisely the zone the framework specifies.

What the four strands collectively establish is not that physics has arrived at the framework's thesis. It is that fundamental physics, independently of cognitive-science input and along four methodologically distinct routes, has converged on observer-dependence at the zone where direct causal contact with the substrate being modeled is unavailable. None of these works identifies the physics-side recognition with the cognitive architecture §II described, the psychiatric mechanism §III characterized, or the philosophy-of-science logical structure §IV developed. The cross-disciplinary bridge — the claim that what all of these literatures describe is structurally one phenomenon — is unoccupied in the literature. §VI takes up that bridge; it carries the argument the rest of the paper turns on.
