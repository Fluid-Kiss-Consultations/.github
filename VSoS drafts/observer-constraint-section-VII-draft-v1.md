§VI closed by handing the application to §VII: a contemporary debate in which the translation-only-zone condition obtains, the generative-and-amortised cognitive architecture operates at the community level, the holism of confirmation underwrites auxiliary-routing, and the amortised-commitment machinery entrenches positions the evidence does not directly adjudicate. The case is the superdeterminism / retrocausality / standard-quantum-mechanics debate. Bell's theorem (Bell 1964) imposes a structural constraint on any local hidden-variable account of quantum correlations, and the constraint is not adjudicable by experimental violation alone: the inequality's derivation rests on a conjunction of assumptions — locality (or continuity of action), measurement statistics, and statistical independence — and an experimental violation of the inequality forces revision somewhere in the conjunction without specifying where. The hidden-variable substrate, where one is posited, is by construction not available to direct causal contact: experiments yield correlation statistics consistent or inconsistent with the inequality, but the variables themselves are accessed only through the theoretical apparatus that posits them. The translation-only-zone condition obtains at the foundational level of the debate, not as a contingent feature of any single interpretive program. What the framework predicts under that condition is the pattern §§II-VI specify: structurally available revision loci, holistically permitted auxiliary deployment, amortised-commitment entrenchment of the central interpretive choice. What the contemporary debate exhibits is that pattern.

Each major camp in the debate preserves a different central commitment and routes the pressure of Bell's constraint to a different auxiliary. Standard quantum mechanics — across its interpretive variants — preserves statistical independence as inviolable and routes the revision into the interpretive layer: the measurement problem (collapse, decoherence, branching), the preferred-basis problem, the ontological status of the state vector, the tail problem in objective-collapse theories, the guidance equation and the Lorentz-invariance question in Bohmian mechanics. Each interpretation preserves its central commitment and absorbs Bell-derived pressure through structurally different auxiliaries. Superdeterminism (Hossenfelder and Palmer 2020) preserves locality and continuity of action and routes the revision to statistical independence itself: the joint distribution of hidden variables and measurement settings is held to be correlated in a structurally specifiable way, and the apparent acausality of Bell-type correlations dissolves into a feature of the joint distribution that no interaction needs to mediate at measurement time. Retrocausality, in the canonical reference Price (2012) supplies, preserves microscopic time-symmetry as the foundational commitment and routes the revision to the temporal direction of the causal arrow at the microphysical level, allowing future measurement settings to bear on present hidden-variable distributions without violating local dynamics. Each of these is a coherent response to Bell's constraint within the holistic frame; none is adjudicated against the others by the empirical record the constraint supplies. The topology of the debate is the topology §VI specified — same evidential pressure, multiple structurally available revision loci, the selection of locus underdetermined by the evidence the constraint provides.

The auxiliary the standard-quantum-mechanics camp preserves — statistical independence — is not, on the framework's reading, a peripheral or technical commitment. Statistical independence underwrites essentially all inferential practice in empirical science outside quantum foundations. Random sampling in clinical and survey research, treatment-arm assignment in randomized controlled trials, the assumption that experimental conditions and participant variables are uncorrelated unless specific mechanism is articulated, the operational design of essentially every controlled experiment in the natural and social sciences — all rest on statistical independence as a background commitment so deep it is rarely articulated, let alone defended, in the work that depends on it. Read through the §III-§VI architecture, statistical independence has the precise profile of an amortised inference at maximum precision-weighting: it is feedforward, computationally cheap, fed into downstream inference without iterative re-derivation, and structurally resistant to local perturbation because its revision would propagate into essentially every other inferential commitment the practitioner holds. The framework predicts that the rate at which the community will revise such a prior, when it is challenged by structural argument from a domain (quantum foundations) the community treats as parochial, will be slower than the evidential history alone predicts; and predicts that the slowness will not register as such from within the architecture, because amortised commitments at this depth are not experienced as commitments but as the conditions under which inference happens at all. The framework's claim is not that any individual physicist is exhibiting the clinical-psychiatric mechanism §III operationalizes; the cognitive default in the translation-only zone is the framework's extension of §III's clinical finding, not the finding itself. The claim is that statistical independence functions in the contemporary community as a maximally entrenched amortised auxiliary, and that its persistence under structural challenge is the phenotype the framework's architecture predicts.

Two further data points concretize the picture. The first: a recurring dismissal of the superdeterminism position is that no concrete model exists, that the position is in-principle gesture rather than physics. Donadi and Hossenfelder (2022) construct a Lindblad-form toy model of local and deterministic wave-function collapse with explicit hidden-variable structure; the model reproduces Born's rule when averaged over the hidden-variable distribution and is structurally testable in low-noise small-system regimes. Whether the model is adequate as a theory of physics is not what the framework adjudicates. What the framework registers is that the empirical existence of the concrete model leaves the dismissal largely unchanged. The dismissal does not retreat to "the model is inadequate for reasons X, Y, Z"; it persists in the form "no serious model exists" or "the position is unscientific" alongside the model's continued availability. This is the entrenchment-rate phenomenology the amortised-commitment account predicts: the disconfirming evidence does not confront the prior iteratively at first contact but confronts the cached frame within which "serious model" is already adjudicated, and the cached frame survives the first-pass disconfirmation. The second: Adlam, Hance, Hossenfelder, and Palmer (2024) — the four-author Taxonomy paper that emerged from the 2022 Bonn workshop on physics beyond quantum mechanics — formalize the landscape of superdeterministic, retrocausal, future-input-dependent, and atemporal positions taxonomically, distinguishing model classes by structural features rather than treating the landscape as a monolithic departure from received foundations. The taxonomic move itself, on the framework's reading, is the pattern §VI characterized at community level: when auxiliary-routing reaches a density that resists evaluation as a single block, the discipline produces taxonomy as the structural acknowledgment that the routing is happening. Adlam et al. do not, in the paper, adopt the framework's diagnostic register; they perform the move the diagnostic register predicts.

§VI para 7 anticipated the most provocative move §VII carries: Hossenfelder's instrumentalist turn (Hossenfelder 2024) read as recognizable-from-within the shape §VI specifies rather than as a position outside it. The reading requires care. Hossenfelder's recent position separates the question of what quantum mechanics describes (the interpretational layer the framework reads as auxiliary-routed) from the question of which empirical predictions the formalism delivers (the layer at which agreement is community-wide), and recommends, as a methodological discipline, declining to treat the interpretational dispute as a question physics is currently equipped to settle. The position is not a withdrawal from the superdeterminism advocacy; it is an explicit clarification of what the advocacy claims and what it does not. On the framework's reading, the instrumentalist turn is the structurally expected move when an observer occupying the translation-only zone recognizes the auxiliary-routing as such: rather than commit further weight to a single interpretive frame whose adjudication the evidence does not supply, the observer narrows the load-bearing claims to what the empirical apparatus directly underwrites. This is recognizable from within the topology §VI specified — not a step out of it, because no such step is structurally available, but a redistribution of weight within the topology toward loci where the evidence does adjudicate. The framework reads this as a coherent move, not as a retreat. (Hossenfelder 2021 supplies the related clarification at the level of nomenclature: that statistical-independence violation does not entail loss of free choice of measurement settings — itself a translation-only-zone correction in which the load-bearing inferential commitment is being relocated from a misidentified auxiliary to its actual location.) The framework does not endorse Hossenfelder's interpretive program. It reads her methodological turn as a recognizable-from-within response to the topology she has been arguing within.

Two clarifications close §VII. The first: the framework does not adjudicate which of standard quantum mechanics, superdeterminism, or retrocausality is correct. It does not supply evidential support for any of them, and it does not refute any of them. What it specifies is the topology of the debate — the structural sense in which each camp's response is coherent within the holistic frame, which auxiliary each preserves and which each routes pressure to, and why the community's rate of revision under structural challenge is what the amortised-commitment architecture predicts rather than what the evidential history alone would predict. §I's scope claim binds: this is a diagnostic framework, not a theory of physics. The reader most likely to mistake the framework for the latter is the reader of §VII, and the disclaimer is therefore most needed here. The second: extending the framework to a contemporary live debate raises the obvious objections — that the diagnostic register pathologizes physicists, that the framework cannot itself be exempt from the architecture it describes, that the framework risks unfalsifiability, that what is being offered is a relabeling of paradigm-stability claims already present in the post-Kuhnian literature. §VIII takes these up.
