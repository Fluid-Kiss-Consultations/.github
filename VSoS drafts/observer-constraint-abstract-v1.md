## Abstract

Self-sealing reasoning structures in certain domains of theoretical physics are formally identical, not merely analogous, to documented clinical mechanisms of delusional ideation. I argue that the identity is not coincidence but a necessary consequence of epistemic underdetermination operating on a causally bounded observer.

The argument proceeds across four layers. Contemporary neuroscience establishes that perception is constructive: the brain is a hierarchical prediction machine generating its model of reality under a free-energy-minimization imperative (Clark 2013; Friston 2010). Clinical psychiatry has operationalized the structure of self-sealing belief under the Bias Against Disconfirmatory Evidence (BADE) paradigm, with meta-analytic support and dimensional extension across non-clinical populations (Moritz & Woodward 2006; Woodward et al. 2007; McLean et al. 2017). Philosophy of science establishes that evidence underdetermines theory: any theory can be maintained against disconfirmation via auxiliary adjustment (Quine 1951; Duhem 1906; Stanford 2006). And within contemporary fundamental physics, the same structure is now being formalized directly: Wolpert, Rovelli, and Scharnhorst (2025) have formalized the circularity underlying the Second Law and the Past Hypothesis, and both Wolfram (2023) and Arsiwalla et al. (2025) have formalized observer-dependent physical law.

No prior work bridges these literatures. I argue the bridge is not merely available but required: the cognitive architecture that constructs reality and the one that theorizes about domains beyond causal detectability are the same architecture. When that architecture operates on domains where observation is possible only through translated causal residue, self-sealing reasoning is not a failure mode — it is the expected output. I illustrate the framework by applying it to the superdeterminism debate in quantum foundations (Hossenfelder & Palmer 2020; Donadi & Hossenfelder 2022; Price 2012), showing the framework is diagnostic of the debate's structure without adjudicating its physics. The framework applies symmetrically across the debate landscape, including to the present author.

## Design notes for review

**Structure:**
- ¶1 (thesis, 2 sentences): claim of formal identity + necessity clause.
- ¶2 (four layers): one sentence per layer, citations compressed. Concludes with the physics layer framed as "the same structure" — this is the unification pivot.
- ¶3 (bridge + application + symmetry): priority claim; argumentative core; application; (D) closure.

**Load-bearing phrases:**
- *"formally identical, not merely analogous"* — forecloses the "it's just a metaphor" dismissal on the first reading.
- *"necessary consequence"* — signals the argument is deductive, not merely suggestive.
- *"causally bounded observer"* — austere replacement for the Perplexity-origin "physiologically causal-ceilinged observer"; carries the same meaning (access restricted to the causal) with less syllabic and conceptual load. The bounding mechanism gets unpacked in Layer 1.
- *"the same structure is now being formalized directly"* — the pivot that ties neuroscience/psychiatry/philosophy to physics without forcing the reader to make the jump themselves.
- *"not a failure mode — it is the expected output"* — the key reframing; shifts the reader's default from deficiency-frame to architecture-frame.
- *"diagnostic of the debate's structure without adjudicating its physics"* — bounds the application and makes the paper unrejectable by physicists who disagree on Bell interpretation.
- *"including to the present author"* — carries (D); forecloses the "this is a takedown" reading; signals the framework is universal and humble.

**What is deliberately absent:**
- No mention of Iterative Mutualism, Bridge Point B, the Great Filter, the three-business singularity, or any downstream work. The paper stands alone per the ratified handoff.
- No mention of the keystone position in the paper series. That's for the introduction, not the abstract.
- No "Dear Sabine" framing. The superdeterminism citations are bounded and neutral.
- No discussion of the BRANDI/SHANNON/NIKOSystem technical work. Wrong paper, wrong voice.

**Citation count in abstract:** 11 distinct references (compressed into clusters). This is at the high end for an abstract but defensible given the four-layer structure. Alternative: reduce to one anchor per layer — Clark 2013; Woodward et al. 2007; Stanford 2006; Wolpert-Rovelli-Scharnhorst 2025; Hossenfelder & Palmer 2020. If the target venue caps citations-in-abstract lower, that's the trim path. Default recommendation: keep current cluster; venues that restrict will flag it at submission.
